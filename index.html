Sustainability Through the Ages

1968

Intel Founded

Intel is founded by Robert Noyce and Gordon Moore, launching a new era of semiconductor innovation.

1971

First Microprocessor

Intel releases the 4004, the world’s first commercial microprocessor.

1978

8086 Processor

The x86 architecture is introduced, shaping modern computing systems.

1985

386 Processor

Intel brings 32-bit computing to personal computers.

2006

Peak Emissions

Intel reaches peak emissions and begins large-scale sustainability efforts.

2020

RISE Strategy

Intel launches RISE to advance climate action and inclusion.

2022

Net-Zero Commitment

Intel commits to net-zero Scope 1 & 2 emissions by 2040.

2023

99% Renewable Energy

Intel achieves 99% renewable electricity usage.

2024

Sustainability Summit

Global leaders unite to shape sustainable semiconductor manufacturing.
